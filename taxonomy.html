How this was built. A four-stage pipeline: (1) download all sources from index.html in a uniform format; (2) elementize — segment the corpus into sentences and distill atomic concepts/rules; (3) relate — TF-IDF cosine similarity plus stance & antonym logic classify equivalence / similarity / contradiction, and agglomerative clustering groups the ideas; (4) visualize this page. Equivalence/contradiction use curated stance labels documenting scholarly debates; similarity edges are discovered automatically from text.

A note on the native language. Each idea's key term / quote — πάντα ῥεῖ, 道可道，非常道, תת… — is authentic text taken from the original sources (and its 🔊 uses that language's voice, with a romanized fallback where no voice exists). The fuller native statement shown beneath it is an AI-generated modern translation — Modern Greek, Mandarin, Hindi, Persian, Modern Hebrew, and Arabic for Egypt & Mesopotamia (whose ancient tongues have no speech synthesis) — offered for reading and pronunciation, not as a sourced original; it may contain occasional phrasing errors.
